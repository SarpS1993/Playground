## Definition used
For this work, **total invested balances** means the sum of:
1. Debt securities / bonds
2. Shares / equity
3. Mutual funds / investment fund shares

Excluded:
- Deposits / cash
- Insurance and pension entitlements
- Real assets such as housing
- Household liabilities

## Methodology

### 1) Germany
Germany is shown on a **broad official stock basis** using Bundesbank household stock positions for Q3 2025.
The three published buckets used are:
- Debt securities = €221.9bn
- Shares and other equity = €1,823.5bn
- Investment fund shares = €1,272.5bn
These sum to €3,317.9bn.

### 2) Italy
Italy is shown on a **harmonised broad-basis estimate** because a directly surfaced Q3 2025 household stock table in the same ready-to-use format was not identified in this pass.

The estimate was built as follows:
- Starting stock base: official 2023 Banca d’Italia household stock positions
- Then add official quarterly net flows for 2024 Q2, 2024 Q3, 2024 Q4, 2025 Q1, 2025 Q2 and 2025 Q3
- The estimate was kept conservative: two small residual items were intentionally not force-allocated to avoid false precision

Locked final Italy numbers:
- Debt securities = ~€457.5bn
- Shares / equity = ~€1,722.0bn
- Mutual funds / investment fund shares = ~€835.8bn
- Total invested balances = ~€3,015.4bn

### 3) Belgium

## Comparability note
This table is **not perfectly harmonised across all countries**.
- Germany is on a broad official stock basis.
- Italy is a broad harmonised estimate.
- Belgium, Netherlands and Spain rely on narrower clean comparable disclosed buckets used for this project.
- Poland uses the broader direct-equity concept explicitly disclosed by NBP.

Accordingly, this table should be interpreted as an **official-source comparison with one transparent harmonised estimate (Italy)** rather than a fully standardised cross-country statistical dataset.
