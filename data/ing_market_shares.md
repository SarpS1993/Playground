## Methodology

**ING Total** = ING AuM (managed investments) + ING AuC (custody/brokerage assets), as reported in ING internal data FY2025.

**Market Size** = country household investment balances, sourced from central bank data where available (NL, BE) or EFAMA Q4 2025 domicile-based fund statistics (DE, ES, IT, PL).

### Caveats

**Germany ⚠ — Market share is materially understated.**
The €2,965.7bn EFAMA figure is dominated by institutional Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs, ~€2,400bn). The retail-comparable household denominator estimated from Bundesbank flow data is approximately €1,200–1,500bn (equity + fund shares held by households). Using this range, ING Germany's implied retail market share would be **~9–11%** — a more meaningful benchmark for a retail bank.

**Belgium** — NBB Q4 2024 household data (investment fund shares €303bn + listed shares €87bn) is the preferred demand-side measure. Higher than the EFAMA domicile figure (€248bn) because it includes foreign-domiciled funds held by Belgian households.

**Netherlands** — DNB Q3 2025 household data (funds €128.5bn + listed shares €69.4bn + bonds €6.7bn). The EFAMA domicile figure (€869bn) is excluded as it is ~86% institutional pension-like AIFs, making it unsuitable as a retail benchmark.

**Spain / Italy / Poland** — EFAMA domicile-based figures used as central bank household-specific data is either pending extraction (ES) or not yet published at sufficient granularity (IT, PL). These figures include some institutional AIF assets and may overstate the retail household market.
