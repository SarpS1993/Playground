Sources & Methodology

Methodology Note

Target Period: FY2025. Data has been updated to reflect FY2025 annual results published through April 2026. Where FY2025 annual reports were not yet available (e.g. DekaBank Q3 2025), the most recent published figure is used and the reporting period is noted.

Scope: Competitor figures are country-specific wherever possible. ING's internal figures are country-level; mixing group-level competitor data would create misleading comparisons. For competitors that operate across multiple countries, the country-segment or country-domiciled figure is used. Where unavailable, the figure is marked as "Group-level" and flagged in the Data Quality tab.

AUM: Household investment fund shares (mutual funds) — F.52 in national accounts. Market AUM sourced from central bank household financial accounts: DE Bundesbank Q3 2025, BE NBB Q2 2025, ES Banco de España Q4 2025, IT Banca d'Italia harmonised estimate Q3 2025, NL DNB Q3 2025, PL NBP Q3 2025. Competitor AUM sourced from individual annual reports and investor releases.

AUC / Brokerage: Household direct equity (listed shares, F.511) + debt securities (bonds, F.3). Same central bank sources as AUM. Competitor AUC from individual reports where disclosed.

Investment Balances: AUM + AUC combined = total household invested balances. Where AUC is unavailable for a competitor, Investment Balances = AUM only.

Market Penetration: ING internal FY2025 figures divided by the central bank household market totals. AUM share = ING fund AUM ÷ market AUM (mutual funds). AUC share = ING AUC ÷ market AUC (shares + bonds). Total share = ING investment balances ÷ total household invested balances.

Fee Income: For pure asset managers and brokers (e.g. DWS, Azimut, Mediolanum, Fineco, VLK, flatexDEGIRO), commission income ≈ investment fee income. For universal banks (e.g. ABN AMRO, KBC, Commerzbank, Santander), the figure reflects total net fee & commission income per segment reporting — this includes payment services, corporate banking fees, and clearing in addition to investment management. Direct comparison with ING's investment-specific fee income should account for this scope difference.

Poland FX: PLN figures converted to EUR at NBP rate 31 Dec 2025 (EUR/PLN 4.2267).
